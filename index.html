British Columbia, Canada

British Columbia, Canada's westernmost province, boasts a diverse landscape defined by its Pacific coastline and the towering Rocky Mountains. 1 It's a land of contrasts, featuring temperate rainforests, arid interior regions, and numerous islands dotting its rugged shoreline, which stretches over 27,000 kilometers. 2 Notably, BC is home to the Great Bear Rainforest, the world's largest intact temperate rainforest, and also holds the record for the highest recorded temperature in Canada.
